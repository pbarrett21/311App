Week of Jan 27 to Feb 2

Week Summary

This week we spoke with the customer to determine their baseline requirements for the application. As stated in the original project description, the customer wishes to create a web application which will provide citizens of a municipality to report non-emergency issues, much like how a 311 line operates in cities across the country. We also established a weekly meeting schedule with the customer to ensure that they are kept in communication with us throughout the process.

Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • The application we are building does not need to have complex features integrated, but needs to have basic functionality that works well. The product we create will ultimately be used as a proof of concept or working demo so that the customer may pursue funding to continue building their idea into an more detailed and profitable product.
  • We are not the only university working on this product for the customer; they have a group of students at a university in Washington working on their own version of the product as well. Of course, we are to work completely individually, but there may be a time in which the customer would like our team to consult with this team during a meeting.
  • Making decisions about the direction of the project should be made mostly by our team, but if a technical question must be answered, we may send the customer a message. Details about the program are up to the team to determine, as long as it fits the requirements previously given by the customer.

Week of Feb 3 to Feb 9

Week Summary

This week we spoke to the customer to mainly discuss the potential styling of the web app, both in terms of layout as well as in coloring, etc. We have been told that the looks of the web app and overall design is something that we will have to determine ourselves, but that we can feel free to consult with them if we have any big issues we need to bring up.

I created two basic wireframes, one that portrays the potential layout from the perspective of a desktop device, as well as a wireframe which portrays how the app might look or be organized from the view of a mobile device. We showed these to the client and it seemed they were okay with that as a preliminary design, but again they reiterated that we have the final say in terms of the design.

The client also gave more insight into the business model they wish to follow with this app later on, and gave us an idea as to how this should influence our design decisions. I have included these notes below.

Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • The client has said they approve of the design we are considering based on the wireframes we have supplied, but want to ensure that we understand we have the freedom to choose the final design. While this is the case, if there are any large or pressing concerns, we should feel free to contact them at any time.
  • The client is looking to have the service provided to smaller municipalities, and would likely be working with each municipality to determine the best pricing model, etc. This being the case, even municipalities who have not purchased the service would receive notifications of issues in their jurisdiction. This works by allowing any citizen, even those within municipalities which have not purchased the software, to report issues to the service. Administrators or contacts in that municipality's government would then be notified that they have outstanding issues being reported by their citizens, and that they can view or interact with them by purchasing the service.
  • While this pricing model could be considered during the creation of the software, the client has specified that we should not make it a primary concern to implement any advanced features such as this. Since this program that we are creating will just be used as a simple demonstration product for investors, these more complex features do not need to be prioritized. However, the product should be fully functional at least at a basic level.

Week of Feb 10 to Feb 16

Week Summary

The primary purpose of this week's meeting was to talk with the client and establish a rough timeline of the development process, from beginning to end. Up to this point, we have only been planning, and it will likely continue this way for a couple more weeks, with the exception of possible basic code skeletons being created for our program. The schedule we have come up with has been posted below.

Rather interestingly, we were told by the client that they are unsure of the future of the project. They performed market research and noticed that there is at least one other company that is developing a competing solution commericially, so they are unsure if they wish to continue. They have said they will decide over the next week after talking with Chartos. As of right now we are considering this a sort of state of limbo.

The rough schedule we have made is below:

  • Week 1: Mainly learning the needed libraries for the project, possibly creating basic templates.
  • Week 2: Begin work on the login system, possibly start work on the main issue board(s).
  • Week 3: Start integration of post ratings, allowing users to vote on important issues.
  • Week 4: This will be midterms week, so less work will be done, but mostly polishing current implementations.
  • Week 5: This week is Spring Break, so there will likely be no development this week.
  • Week 6: Begin work on the admin back end, as well as running the code through GitHub.
  • Week 7: Most of the actual development work should be done by this point, so this will be mostly polishing.
  • Week 8: Finish work on the admin back end, lots of testing, and possible implementation of extra features.

Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • We established a (very) rough timeline forthe project. The client says this looks good with them, and that as long as we feel we are making sufficient progress then they have no issues with how we decide to go about the process or how we divide our time.
  • The client has essentially put this project on hold after doing market research and determining that there are companies that are currently working on competing commericial products. Because of this, they do not know if they wish to continue. Because of this uncertainty, we will wait over the next days to see if they reach a decision and consult with Prof. Chartos to see which direction he thinks we need to go.

Week of Feb 24 to Mar 2

Week Summary

This week was mainly a progress update for Morgan to know where we are and what progress we have made over the last week. We told him that we have pretty much settled on Angular and Node for this project, more specifically the MEAN (MongoDB, Express, Angular, NodeJS) stack. Choosing this stack will allow a more convenient solution that not only provides Angular and Node as we had already established, but also provides MongoDB, which will prove useful as a database engine for actually storing items such as issues, user accounts, etc.

Brain joined the call towards the end, and we told him that we had settled on Node. We also confirmed that we would likely be needing the Amazon AWS server instance that he offered us previously, and this information was sent to us via Asana.

Morgan noted that next week he would like us to join a Zoom meeting with the student team at Washington State University so that we can communicate with them to determine where they are, how they are approaching the problem, etc. Despite this call, we will of course be working completely separately. This call is simply to bounce ideas off of each other and get another point of view. Zoom meeting details and invites will be sent out via Asana or email.

Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • We updated Morgan and Brain on the progress we have made, which included pushing basic template code and settling on using NodeJS and the MEAN stack.
  • We confirmed to Brain that we would likely be needing the Amazon AWS server instance he has offered before. He said he would be sending these details to us soon.
  • Morgan has said he would like us to join a Zoom meeting next week with the student team at Washington State University. He said he would be sending these details out to us. Week of Mar 3 to Mar 9

Week Summary

This week we met in a Zoom meeting with Morgan, "Brain", as well as the other team from Washington University. We compared the progress we have made on our project with the WU team, and it appears that we are largely at the same point in our projects. Just as we have, their team just finished and submitted a detailed project planning document, and will soon start the actual coding for their application. The WU team has said they currently plan to take less of a web app approach, contrary to our team's methods. The client seems pleased with either approach, so long as it is still functional.

We also compared our methods for user authentication/validation, and it seems that the WU approach is to allow anyone, registered or not, to post and interact with the site. Our current plan is to allow guests to browse issues on the site without logging in, but interacting with the issues, doing things such as posting or voting, will require them to log in. This is mainly to prevent spam and people voting on an issue multiple times (one vote per account).

We have decided, along with the client, that we will not be meeting on conference calls March 10-17, as it is Spring Break. We will resume calls with the client on March 24.

Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • We met with WU team to determine where they are in the project - they are in a comparable spot as us (just finishing planning).
  • Our approach is to require users to log in if they want to post issues, WU is less focused on user logins at this point.
  • Meetings will be suspended for Spring Break but will resume March 24.
