Week of Jan 7 to Feb 2

Week Summary

This week we established a meeting scheduled to meet on a weekly basis via a conference call. I was unfortunately unable to be on the first conference call because I had prior responsibilities on this day. I did, however, get notes and brought up to speed by my teammates.

This is the set of questions we came up with as a team. I was unable to be at the meeting for each question, even if they were not answered.

What source control can we use?
	A: No Answer, Our Team decided that GitHub would be the best solution for us.

Did you want two separate native apps or a web app that will work universally across all platforms? Tell them the reason we think a web app would be the best is because with a web app we can cover all bases at once, Android, iOS, Windows, mac, everything. If someone reports it on their phone than the clerk can log in and see what the report says and issue what’s necessary.
A: No Answer, We have started toying around with the idea about using a web stack such as MEAN. Mongo DB, Express JS, Angular JS, and Node JS. Due to the scopre of the project, native applications did not make sense.

Do you have a graphical designer for how it should look or did you have a color palette in mind? Mention that they threw out a bunch technology stacks but we really wanted to narrow down exactly what we are doing.
A: No Answer, we decided that we would do the best that we could with the design and implemention of the application

Really figure out what their expectations are, that’s something we need to figure out. Because we don’t want them thinking they will have a 100% full functioning, beautifully designed, infinitely scaleable application.
A: Their expectations are the middle of the road. They had a team last year that let them down, and did not deliver. We are thinking in our mind that we are going to deliver an MVP application because we cannot promise something out of the scope of time.\

Ask them how they are wanting to monetize exactly? I.e. could it be charging the cities a relatively small amount and not showing advertisements to users?
A: He doesn’t expect it to be a final product. We won’t necessarily have to figure out this part yet. They expect this to be a first draft to be scrapped and redone later if it presents well to a hedge fund.

Do you have any general idea of what milestones to aim for?
A: It’s up to us. Milestones aren’t really set up. Basically, meet the milestones and time frames presented in class. 

Does Brain have a time that we could contact him?
A: He can jump in the calls if he’s needed. He said he’d rather not get him involved if possible.

One Issue: Brain is the one to ask technical questions. It is up to us to decide how to implement it. As long as it matches the general idea, we can do it our way. Also, another team in Washington state already tried to do this same project and failed. They didn’t take it seriously or something so Brain isn’t going to try to help this time. So it is up to us.

Notes/what he(Morgan) said: The main gist behind is that most municipalities out here don’t have apps that focus on non-emergency situations. They want to come up with a generalized app that any city and municipality can use at a reasonable price. NY had to pay 250k for theirs. This app will cut out redundancy and generalize the process. There could be scope creep. We need it to be geared towards the citizen, nothing more, nothing less. Most people won’t use all of the features. Main idea is to create a working product for them to show off so they can get funding.

General Notes for this week's meeting are below:

  • The application does not have to be complex.
  • We are not the only university working on this product
  • We are developing a MVP for the customer to show to investors to help the real product gain traction.
  • Making decisions are mostly left to us, however any technical questions we can ask via a task software called Asana.
  • The requirements that we have been given, allow for free reign. I believe that it is preferred to deliver a more simplistic application rather than a complicated application.

Week of Feb 3 - Feb 10

Week Summary

This weeks meeting we had a conference call with Morgan. From this we confirmed that we have creative freedom over the design of our application. This week we need to create a schedule to start developing our application. We need to choose a technology stack to work with also. We also need to create our requirements document with deliverables. Notes for this week's meeting are below:
